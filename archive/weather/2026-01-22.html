German Weather Service - Regional Weather Advisory Service Munich, issued
on Wednesday, January 21, 2026, at 2:30 p.m.

Weather forecast for the German Alps:

Between a low-pressure system west of the British Isles and a high-pressure zone over Eastern Europe, an inversion situation with relatively dry air has developed in the Bavarian Alps.

Mostly clear skies overnight into Thursday. Temperatures will drop to -5 to -10 degrees Celsius in the valleys, and locally even lower.

Thursday will be mostly sunny and dry. Temperatures in Alpine valleys will range from 0 to +6 degrees Celsius. At 1500 meters, temperatures will be around +4 degrees Celsius, and at 3000 meters, down to -5 degrees Celsius. Winds will remain light, averaging 20 km/h, with gusts up to 50 km/h in exposed locations, blowing from the east.


Further outlook until Saturday, January 24, 2026: Friday will be mostly cloudy, but generally dry. Highs will range from 0°C in the Berchtesgaden region to +6°C in the Upper Allgäu. Temperatures will reach around +1°C at 1500 meters and down to -7°C at 3000 meters. Saturday will be mostly cloudy with some snow in places. Highs will range between 0°C and +4°C, with the highest temperatures in the Allgäu. Temperatures will be around 0°C at 1500 meters and down to -8°C at 3000 meters. Winds will be mostly light from the east on Friday and Saturday.
